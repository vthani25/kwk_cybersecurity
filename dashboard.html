Insights

  • Social Engineering is the most reported vulnerability, making up the largest portion of incidents in the dataset, followed closely by unpatched software and weak passwords.
  • Financial losses are unevenly distributed across regions. A small number of countries account for a disproportionate share of global cyberattack losses. This indicates concentrated high-impact activity.
  • Unpatched software incidents showed a sharp increase between 2020 and 2023. This aligns with the remote work transition period due to quarantine and reduced IT patching capacity during that time.
  • Defense mechanisms are not evenly adopted. Traditional tools like antivirus and firewalls remain dominant, while AI-based detection shows lower representation. This could suggest that newer technologies are being adapted at a slower pace, or that cybersecurity is not at risk by AI.
